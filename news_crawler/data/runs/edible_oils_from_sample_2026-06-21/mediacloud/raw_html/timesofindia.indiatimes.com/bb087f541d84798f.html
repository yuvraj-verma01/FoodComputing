FDA seizes untested soyabean oil

Soumitra Bose / Oct 09, 2025, 00:57 IST
Share
AA
Text Size
  • Small
  • Medium
  • Large
Nagpur: With Diwali's festive fervour around the corner, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has launched a crackdown in and around Nagpur on shops selling refined soyabean oil without the mandatory laboratory setup for testing to ensure consumer safety.In a series of raids, FDA teams stormed outlets in Chhapru Nagar and Gondia, seizing massive stocks of untested oil that flouted food safety regulations. The operation, led by joint commissioner, food, KR Jaipurkar targeted vendors lacking proper laboratory setups, and Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) approvals.In Nagpur, authorities confiscated 824 litres of suspect soyabean oil, valued at 1.24 lakh, from outlets operating without mandatory testing facilities. Meanwhile, in Gondia, a staggering 2,144 litres worth 3.23 lakh were seized by the vigilance team of FDA under joint commissioner Rahul Khade and assistant commissioner Abhay Deshpande.These raids underscore the FDA's zero-tolerance policy toward vendors bypassing quality checks, a critical measure to prevent adulterated oils from tainting festive meals. The seized oils, sold without mandatory checks and testing, posed potential health hazards, especially during Diwali when demand for cooking oil soars.
