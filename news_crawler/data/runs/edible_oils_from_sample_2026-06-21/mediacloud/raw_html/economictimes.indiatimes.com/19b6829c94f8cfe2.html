    Noida: 500 kg fake paneer found before Diwali. Here's how to check the purity and what you can do to save yourself

    Synopsis

    Noida authorities intercepted 500 kg of adulterated paneer, smelling foul and unfit for consumption, en route to Delhi-NCR markets. Seized ahead of Diwali, the product was safely destroyed. Experts urge consumers to check for freshness and report suspicious items to FSSAI to prevent health risks during the festive season.

    Noida authorities have seized 500 kilograms of adulterated paneer ahead of Diwali. The paneer, which reportedly had a foul smell, was being transported from Jangi Milk Plant in Mewat, Haryana, to markets in Delhi-NCR. The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) intercepted the vehicle before the products reached consumers. During a late-night check on 11-12 October 2025, FSSAI officials stopped a vehicle carrying paneer that appeared spoiled and unfit for consumption. The inspection team, including Mukesh Kumar, Vijay Bahadur Patel, and Ravindra Nath Verma, collected samples for laboratory testing, a TOI report stated.

    Seized paneer destroyed

    The 500 kilograms of paneer were stored at New Garhwal Dairy in Bhangel, Noida, before being destroyed the next day with the support of local authorities. FSSAI’s Deputy Commissioner Sarvesh Kumar said, “This action has been taken to protect public health. During the festival season, the supply of adulterated products increases. Strict monitoring is necessary.”

    How to check paneer purity

    Experts advise checking paneer before buying or consuming it. Fresh paneer should have a mild smell, firm texture, and white colour. Paneer that feels sticky, has an unusual colour, or emits a bad smell may be adulterated. Avoid buying products from unverified sources or street vendors.


    Ways to Identify Fake Paneer

    1. Check the Smell

      • Fresh paneer has a mild, milky aroma.

      • If it smells sour, chemical-like, or unusually strong, it may be adulterated.

    2. Look at the Color

      • Genuine paneer is white or off-white.

      • Yellowish, dull, or unevenly colored paneer could be fake.

    3. Feel the Texture

      • Real paneer is soft, slightly springy, and holds its shape.

      • Sticky, mushy, or overly hard paneer may be adulterated.

    4. Water Test

      • Drop a small piece of paneer in water.

      • Fresh paneer sinks slowly. Paneer with excessive starch, chemicals, or oil may float.

    5. Taste Carefully

      • Real paneer tastes mild and creamy.

      • Bitter, soapy, or chemical taste is a warning sign.

    6. Check for Excess Oil

      • Adulterated paneer may release oil on pressing or cooking due to refined oils or chemicals.

    7. Buy from Trusted Sources

      • Always purchase from verified shops or branded dairies.

      • Avoid street vendors or unlabelled products.

    8. Check Expiry and Labelling

      • Packaged paneer should have manufacturing and expiry dates.

      • Missing labels or vague information may indicate fake products.

        Earlier, in Shamli district, authorities raided a factory producing paneer using refined oil and chemicals. Large quantities of fake dairy products were seized. Experts warn that consuming adulterated paneer can cause food poisoning, liver issues, and intestinal diseases, especially for children and the elderly.

        FSSAI continues inspections ahead of Diwali

        FSSAI has announced that inspections across districts will continue through Diwali. Officers will regularly check shops selling dairy products, sweets, and namkeen. Immediate action will be taken wherever adulteration or poor-quality products are found. Sarvesh Kumar urged the public to report any suspicious products to the food department.
