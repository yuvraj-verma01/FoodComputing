KOLKATA: Kolkata Municipal Corporation is set to undertake a drive to check adulteration in food ahead of the festive season. Food inspectors will start visiting restaurants across the city to check the use of food colour and spices in cooking.
The primary concern though is the quality of water. A drive will also be conducted to check whether potable drinking water is used for cooking.
"Food inspectors will check if the ingredients used conform to Food Safety and Standards Authority of India. Food samples are being collected from restaurants and tested at KMC laboratories," a health department official said.
From tests carried out so far, some mid-category restaurants have been found using adulterated spices. Also, quality of cooking oil has been dodgy at shacks selling rolls and chowmein. However, most of the well known restaurants are conscious about quality and hygiene.
Department officials said the teams would carry out checks in stalls near 
Durga Puja pandals till at least shashthi. "We have set up a special team of 18 food inspectors who will be doing the rounds of food stalls that are set up around renowned pandals in the city," said an official. 
"We will send our inspectors to popular restaurants in specific zones (Shyambazar-Hatibagan belt, Sealdah, College Street, Bhowanipore, Kidderpore, Hazra, Deshapriya Park, Gariahat, Jodhpur Park, Park Circus, Behala, Tollygunge among others), where pandal hoppers throng during the festive days. Earlier, KMC used to collect samples but couldn't detect food adulteration. With the laboratory upgrade, it will now be possible," the official added.
