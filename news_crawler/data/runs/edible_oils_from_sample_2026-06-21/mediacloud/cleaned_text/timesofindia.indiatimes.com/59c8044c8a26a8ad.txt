Noida: Over 42 kg of Italian white bread loaves without a mandatory expiry or âuse by' date were seized from a leading fast food chain outlet, renowned for its sandwiches in Sector 121, Noida, after the food safety department intensified inspections across GB Nagar ahead of New Year celebrations.
The assistant commissioner (food), Sarvesh Kumar, said, "The absence of expiry details amounts to a serious violation of food safety norms. A show-cause notice has been issued to the manufacturing unit supplying the bread." He added that food items without proper labelling or expiry details pose a direct risk to consumers and clear information on shelf life is mandatory under the Food Safety and Standards Act. 
Kumar said the action was part of a coordinated enforcement drive underway ahead of New Year celebrations in the city. Multiple teams from the food safety department inspected and collected samples from restaurants, cafes and fast-food chains across Noida, Greater Noida and the Jewar area.
On Monday and Tuesday, officials collected samples of reused cooking oil, bread and dough, pizzas, rice-based preparations, milk and dairy products, sauces and beverages from well-known food outlets operating in malls, markets and highway locations. 
Inspections were conducted at Gaur City Mall in Greater Noida West, DLF Mall of India and surrounding eateries in Sector 18, Noida, as well as commercial belts in sectors 62, 74, 121, 132 and 135. Food outlets in Greater Noida, Knowledge Park and along the Yamuna Expressway were also checked.
"In all, 26 food samples were collected during the two-day drive and were sent to the laboratory for testing," Kumar said. "If any sample is found to be unsafe or non-compliant with prescribed standards, strict action will be taken against the concerned establishment, including penalties and prosecution, as per the law."
He added that enforcement action continued late into the night on both days, reflecting the administration's focus on preventive checks ahead of New Year gatherings. Improvement notices were issued at several establishments where deficiencies related to hygiene, storage practices or labelling were noticed.
During inspections on Dec 31, officials found hygiene-related deficiencies at several establishments, including food courts and standalone restaurants at Venice Mall and GIP Mall, as well as eateries in sectors 62 and 129. 
"These checks will continue through the New Year period so that residents and visitors are served safe and standard food during celebrations," Kumar said.
