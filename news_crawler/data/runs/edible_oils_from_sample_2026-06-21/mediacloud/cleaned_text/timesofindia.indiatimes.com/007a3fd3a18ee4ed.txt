According to media reports, a major breakthrough in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, came on January 2, 2025, when police uncovered a factory engaged in the illegal production of fake ghee. The factory was found mixing hazardous ingredients like urea, palm oil, and synthetic essences to replicate renowned dairy brands. Authorities seized an astonishing 25,500 kilograms of counterfeit ghee, along with raw materials used in its production, marking a significant step against food adulteration.
As reported by India Today Video Desk, the factory was operating under the name âShyam Agro.â According to Jagran, the factory was located in a tin shed in the Tajganj area on Shamsabad Road. During the raid, officials seized large quantities of counterfeit ghee, along with raw materials such as palm oil, urea, refined oils, and Vanaspati ghee.
In a video shared by India Today, Suraj Kumar Rai, Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP) City, revealed shocking details about the operation. He stated, âWe discovered packaging materials from 18 brands along with large quantities of essence, urea, palm oil, refined oils of various types, and Vanaspati ghee.â These ingredients were used to create counterfeit ghee, which was then packed to imitate popular brands. The operation deceived consumers by selling these harmful, low-quality products at premium prices, posing serious health risks.
The counterfeit ghee was manufactured at an incredibly low cost of just â¹175 per kilogram but was being sold to unsuspecting consumers for as much as â¹650 per kilogram. To deceive buyers, the product was packaged with labels of well-known dairy brands such as Amul.
According to Jagran, during the raid, police uncovered 50 tins branded as Amul, all set to be transported to Meerut, highlighting the extensive distribution network of the operation. Although the factory was registered in Gwalior, it operated in Agra and maintained several storage facilities throughout the city. The fake ghee was supplied to multiple districts and even other states, demonstrating the scale of the illegal activity.
As reported by Jagran, the police arrested five people during the raid, including the factory manager. The investigation is still ongoing, and the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) is now involved to make sure strict legal action is taken against those responsible.
Suraj Kumar Rai explained that the factory made fake ghee, labeled it with popular brand names, and then distributed it to the market. This shows how big the operation was and how it was designed to trick people into buying fake products. The investigation is still underway, and officials are trying to identify others involved in the distribution network.
According to the reports, legal action is expected against those involved, with charges likely under the Food Safety and Standards Act. Authorities are also working to make sure stricter rules are put in place to stop such fraud from happening again.
