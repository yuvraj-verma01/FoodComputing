Kanpur: The Food Safety and Drug Administration (FSDA) carried out a major raid on Friday at M/s Gayatri Oil Industry in Fazalganj, seizing approximately 35,727 litres of edible oil worth Rs53 lakh after the Food Safety on Wheels (FSW) test found the samples suspicious.
Officials discovered that the oil containers had fresh stickers pasted over old ones. The entire stock stored in the godown was seized for further investigation. 
Assistant Commissioner (Food-II) Sanjay Pratap Singh said another FSDA team led by a food safety officer raided Satyam Sweet House in Maharajpur and destroyed 75 kilograms of substandard cheese worth Rs22,500. In a separate operation, officials raided Reouna village in Ghatampur tehsil and destroyed 240 kilograms of mawa made from substandard materials. Around 10kg vegetable fat worth Rs2,704 were also seized from the site. 
Singh said the department has launched a special campaign against adulteration and the sale of substandard food items to ensure the availability of pure and safe food for citizens. The drive is being conducted under the directions of the Food Commissioner. Various FSDA teams collected 25 food samples, including refined rice bran oil, mustard oil, mixed milk, cheese, mawa, and skimmed milk powder from different parts of the city.The department also deployed a mobile testing lab (FSW) and DOM machine to conduct on-the-spot food testing.
Food vendors were instructed to maintain hygiene, use standard ingredients, and clearly display the shelf life and "best before" dates on milk products to ensure transparency and consumer safety.
