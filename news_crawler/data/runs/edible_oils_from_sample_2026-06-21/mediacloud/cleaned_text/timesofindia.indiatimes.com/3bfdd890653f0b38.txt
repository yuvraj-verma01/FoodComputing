Tuticorin: Officials of the food safety department seized as many as 386 packs of ice cream of a popular brand from a shop at Kovilpatti in Tuticorin district on Tuesday for failing to carry details about milk fat and edible oil content on labels. 
A team led by S Mariappan, designated food safety officer of Tuticorin, inspected the outlet run by a private agency and found that the shop violated Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) regulations as some of packs, including cones and sandwiches, did not contain proper labels.
FSSAI norms dictate that milk fat and edible oil content must be displayed on labels and if the product uses edible oil, it must be mentioned as âfrozen dessertsâ so that consumers could know. 
Mariappan said the private agency did not have proper documents and is suspected to have obtained its food licence by using false information. So, the licence of outlet has been suspended as well. Officials have warned the public to be wary and not to buy ice cream packs which do not display milk fat and edible oil content. They also urged consumers to file a complaint by calling 94440-42322 or at https://foodsafety.tn.gov.in or via TN food safety app if they come across any such illegal food products. Inspections will continue for the next few days in Tuticorin to stop the sale of ice cream packs without proper labels.
