Kanpur: The Food Safety and Drug Administration Department (FSDA) on Thursday seized a large consignment of edible oil of substandard quality and other food items during a citywide inspection drive.
According to officials, a total of 19,944 litres of edible oil worth Rs 30,22,468 was seized from Guru Nanak Oil Traders after the Food Safety on Wheels (FSW) mobile testing unit declared the oil sample as suspicious.
In a parallel operation, food safety officers confiscated approximately 1,500 kg of substandard cardamom seeds and âgatta' from three business establishments where the food products were being manufactured in unhygienic conditions. Another team seized 1,350 kg of adulterated pulses valued at Rs 54,000 being prepared in similar conditions.
Assistant Commissioner (Food-II) Sanjay Pratap Singh said the department has launched a special campaign against the sale of substandard and adulterated food items to ensure citizens receive pure and healthy food. The drive is being conducted under the directions of the food commissioner. 
Officers further instructed vendors to maintain hygiene and use only unadulterated, standard ingredients in food preparation. TNN
