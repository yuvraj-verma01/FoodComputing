Raipur: The food and drug administration department have launched a massive crackdown on adulterated food items, seizing paneer worth over Rs 14.63 lakh.
The dept collected samples from the Durg division to check for compliance with the 
Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006. Including 17 samples from Durg district, 11 from Rajnandgaon and Khairagarh-Chhuikhadan-Gandai, 16 from Bemetara, 18 from Balod, and eight from Kabirdham. All samples have been sent for analysis. Action against adulteration In Rajnandgaon, authorities destroyed 460 kg of paneer at Raunak Enterprises, Paneka, after finding it was being manufactured using skimmed milk powder and palm oil. A case was also registered against a mega mart for stocking expired products.
In Raipur, officials inspected Gopi Dairy and Sweets in Boriakhurd area. They destroyed 200 kg of sweets produced under unsanitary conditions and seized 900 kg of khoya kalakand and Krishna barfi valued at Rs 1.75 lakh. The total estimated value of these seizures stands at Rs 14,63,500. Legal samples from these stocks have been sent for laboratory testing, and further action will be taken under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006 (Rules 2011) once the reports are received.
Mobile testing and surveillance Under the surveillance plan of the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI), samples of dry fruits, including dates and raisins, along with various chocolates, were collected from different outlets. Mobile food testing laboratories have been deployed across the division for spot inspections. These units tested 506 samples in Durg, 68 in Bemetara, 56 in Balod, and 119 in Kabirdham.
