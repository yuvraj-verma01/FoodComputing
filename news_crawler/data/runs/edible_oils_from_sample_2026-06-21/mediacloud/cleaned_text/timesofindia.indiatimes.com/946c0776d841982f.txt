Agra:Ahead of Diwali, officials have stepped up efforts to curb food adulteration. The Food Safety and Drug Administration (FSDA) carried out swift operations across districts to ensure that residents receive safe and pure festive delicacies. In the Mansoorpur area of Muzaffarnagar, the FSDA team intercepted a vehicle on the highway carrying around 15 quintals of adulterated paneer worth nearly Rs 3.3 lakh; the product, allegedly headed to Uttarakhand for sale, was seized and later destroyed by burying it in the ground using a JCB. Officials also collected four samples for laboratory testing.
Food inspector Archana Deheeran told TOI, "Paneer was being brought from Aligarh to supply Uttar Pradesh; samples have been collected, and the entire paneer that was in bad condition was also destroyed. Apart from this, we also destroyed 'khiya' (reduced dry milk). The drive is ongoing due to upcoming festivals." Mahendra Singh, the assistant commissioner of FSDA Agra, said, "During the inspection, around five shops were issued improvement notices for hygiene violations."
Chief food safety officer Atul Pathak said, "Information was received that a large quantity of oil was being illegally transported. Acting on the information, a team arrived at the scene. 
The warehouse owner is currently absconding. The warehouse has been closed, and a notice is being posted. Once the owner arrives, oil samples will be sent for testing. If found guilty, strict action will be taken."
FSDA Agra conducted inspections across several locations in the district, collecting 11 food samples and destroying over 2.2 tonnes of substandard food items. During the enforcement drive, officials seized and destroyed 1,725 kg of khoya, 500 kg of paneer and 30 kg of artificially coloured petha worth around Rs 4.48 lakh, which were being stored or transported under unhygienic conditions.
In addition, 800 kg of edible oil â including 600 kg of mustard oil and 200 kg of refined soybean oil â worth about Rs 1.3 lakh was seized from local shops. Samples were also collected from well-known establishments, and two samples of sugar toys were taken for testing. The FSDA has sent all 11 samples for laboratory analysis, and further legal action will follow under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006.
