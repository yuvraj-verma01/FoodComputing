Ludhiana: Health department authorities launched an intensive food inspection and sampling drive across Ludhiana this week, seizing nine different food articles to verify safety and quality standards ahead of the busy festive season.
The operation, led by civil surgeon Dr Ramandeep Kaur, focused on high-demand items like sweets and dairy products commonly consumed during holiday celebrations. The drive was carried out by the food safety team under the supervision of designated officer Dr Sandeep Singh.
Samples Taken Across the City
The Food Safety Team collected a total of nine samples from various localities, including Gill Road, Kakowal Road, and Basant Park. The seized items were specifically chosen for testing due to their frequent use in festive preparations. Samples included sweets like mango delight, two samples of chana laddoo, Jammu chocolate barfi, and gujhia; along with dairy or fats such as two samples of desi ghee, paneer, and used cooking oil.
All samples have been sent to the State Food Laboratory for detailed analysis to determine if they comply with Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) regulations. 
Dr Ramandeep Kaur emphasized the Health Department's commitment to ensuring the supply of safe, hygienic, and high-quality food for Ludhiana residents. "Appropriate legal action will be taken as per the laboratory reports once results are received," the department stated.
Dr Ramandeep Kaur appealed to all food business operators (FBOs) to strictly maintain hygiene, use only good quality raw materials, and adhere to all FSSAI guidelines. The Food Safety Team confirmed that these inspection and sampling drives will continue across the district throughout the festive season to prevent adulteration and safeguard public health while consumption of sweets and dairy products is at its peak.
