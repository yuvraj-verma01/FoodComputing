Analogue paneer – made primarily from vegetable oil and other ingredients like cheap milk powder, maize flour and maida – is used as a substitute of traditional paneer. It is cheaper by about 50%.
It is legally allowed to be sold in India by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI).
“If we can control the use of synthetic paneer, it will help to increase the demand for paneer made from milk and in turn increase the demand for milk,” Doodh Utpadak Prakriya Vyavsayik Kalyankari Sangh, an association of dairies from private and cooperative sector of Maharashtra, wrote in a letter to Narhari Jhirwal, state food and drug administration (FDA) minister.
The letter also highlighted the growing incidence of adulteration in milk, ghee, and khoya.
“We have requested the government to increase awareness among people about fake paneer. The industry has also decided to launch awareness campaigns,” Kutwal said.
The state government is also working on amending the law to prevent adulteration of milk at source, industry insiders told ET.
Analogue paneer is mostly manufactured by the unorganised sector and some small regional dairies.
